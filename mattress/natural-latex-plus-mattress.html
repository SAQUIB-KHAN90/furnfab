Latex vs Foam Mattress – What’s the Difference?

When shopping for a new mattress, many people find themselves torn between latex and foam options. While both offer their own set of advantages, some key differences can help you decide. The latex used in latex mattresses is obtained from the sap of rubber trees, thereby making it a sustainable and environmentally friendly product. On the other hand, foam mattresses are typically made from synthetic materials like polyurethane, which are less environmentally friendly.

In terms of bounce and responsiveness, latex mattresses stand out with their naturally springy feel. Foam mattresses tend to be denser and less responsive, which some users may find too firm or slow to adjust. When it comes to cooling and breathability, latex has excellent air circulation, helping regulate temperature throughout the night. Foam mattresses, particularly traditional memory foam, tend to trap heat and may feel warmer during summer months. Durability is another big plus for latex. There is minimal sagging in latex mattresses throughout 10 to 15 years if they are made of high-quality latex. Foam mattresses usually show signs of wear and sagging after 5 to 7 years. Lastly, in terms of environmental impact, latex wins with its natural, biodegradable makeup, while foam mattresses contribute more to synthetic waste.

Overall, if you’re looking for a mattress that offers long-term value, better airflow, and natural support, a latex rubber mattress is a better choice compared to standard foam.
